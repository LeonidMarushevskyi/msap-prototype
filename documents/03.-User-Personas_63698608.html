BIO

Ruby is a housewife. She is married to Luke Cumar (28). She has two birth children (Ava, a girl of 5 years old, and Emma, a girl of 7 years old). She received her foster care license (ALE897C24) last year.  She has two children placed with her in foster care (Stella (6, girl) and Lucas (9, boy)). She likes to help children thrive and cares about her community. When her kids are at school, she volunteers at the local library and keeps up her household tasks (cleaning her home, shopping for the kids, preparing meals).  She also browses the Internet. She is currently considering taking a part-time cashier job to earn some extra income for the holidays. 

She is looking at a job that has afternoon hours two days a week. Luke can come home early on one day to support the kids but not the other. On the other day, he can pick the kids up from child care at 5:30 pm. Ruby is looking for a child care provider who can take all 4 kids for after school time frame but just one day a week.

BIO

Margaret is a waitress. She gave birth when she was 23. She didn't have a job, was alone and had problems with alcohol. Her waitress job had her leaving her children alone at night. She is the birth mom of twins: Sally (9, girl) and Zane (9, boy). The stress of not much income made her violent with her kids. She also resorted to alcohol to relieve the stress. Her kids were taken from her because of these factors and calls received reporting that she was violent and hurt them.  Now she wants to have them returned to her and she is doing all that is possible for this to happen. She tries to stay in contact with her kids while she works toward more stability in her life and proving that she can be a better parent.

The caseworker is willing to have the children return home, if, as part of the safety plan, there is child care for her two kids. The caseworker is searching for child care during the second shift.
